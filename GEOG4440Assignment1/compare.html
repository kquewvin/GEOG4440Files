When viewing the maps side by side, you can easily make a distinction of the temporal and spatial differences between these two images. Firstly, I would like to mention that both these images were taken in March within the early spring season. However, by observing these images, it seems that the 2021 image appears to have more vegetation coverage compared to the 2002 image despite having more developed infrastrucure.

Unfortunately, I was unable to perform an NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) analysis to quantify vegetation greenness to gather information about vegetation density and plant health. An NDVI analysis essentially measures the reflected red and near-infrared electromagnetic wavelengths from plant chlorophyll. Because plants are generally good absorbers of visible light - except green (500-570 nm) - and good reflectors for infrared light, we can infer that healthy and high densities of vegetation would result in a higher NDVI value. The images I obtained only provided bands within the visible spectrum.
