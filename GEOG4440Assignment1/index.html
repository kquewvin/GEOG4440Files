Sources I used to find free remote sensing data and imagery:
USGS Earth Explorer
The United States Geological Survey provides geographic data about Earth's land, water, and atmosphere. It collects, monitors, and analyzes scientific data to understand natural processes. It is an open access portal for downloading free aerial imagery, including digital elevation models, Landsat, LiDAR, MODIS, and more.

Scholars Geoportal
The Scholars GeoPortal provides access to large scale geospatial datasets. It allows for searching, discovery, and visualization to make data about Ontario
for the public.

City of Toronto - Open Data
The City of Toronto's open data portal provides free and downloadable information about the city. Accessible data can vary broadly from textual data like census reports
to geospatial data about infrastructure, planning, and boudaries.

Sentinel 2 Archive
Provides open access to EU Copernicus Sentinel-1, Sentinel-2, Sentinel-3 and Sentinel-5P aerial data.

National Snow and Ice Data Centre
Provides open access to cryospheric and geophysical data. It is suitable for identifying changes to global snow coverage, detection, and temporal changes in the environment.

Data Acquisition

The dataset that I decided to use was obtained through the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (2002) and First Base Solutions (2021) on Scholars GeoPortal's open data hub. The scope of this area was selected using the polygon clipping tool that is available to use by selecting to download by an area of interest. I am particularly interested in urban development and expansion of cities. As such, I found and selected data that is most revelant to my research. York University's Keele campus was my area of interest for this assignment because the campus is located within the center of the Greater Toronto Area and just north of the Toronto metropolis, bordering the York region. The University has undergone various development and changes within the past two decades and is still continuing development.



After downloading the data, I had to open each individual .tif file into CATALYST Professional. The original .tif files were incompatible with CATALYST and so I had to use the translate tool to convert them into PCIDSK (.pix) files and export the entire dataset as one file. I did this for both maps.
